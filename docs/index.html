The problem

The EU AI Act, NYC Local Law 144, the Colorado AI Act, China's algorithm rules, and Japan's guidelines each impose different, moving duties on the same AI system. Most "compliance" tooling answers with a checklist or a slide — assertions you take on faith. This answers with evidence anyone can verify.

What makes a finding governable

  1. Point-in-time law — audited against the statute as it stood on a given date.
  2. Signed, hash-chained ledger — re-derive every hash and signature yourself.
  3. Warrant-gated chain of custody — no intake without a valid, served authorization.
  4. Anti-cherry-pick proofs — commit-then-sample detects omitted or skewed evidence.

One chain ties it together: authorization → commitment → custody → finding.

Honest about its limits

Code can make the exercise of authority auditable; it cannot create it. This does not grant statutory power to compel a company's data, and it cannot prove a committed population is the true universe without compelled access or external cross-checks. It is the instrument that's ready the moment that authority exists — and, short of that, the most rigorous pre-deployment auditor available.
