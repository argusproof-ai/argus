## Recommendation (one line)

**Lead with open-source + influence now (≈$0), targeting the emerging independent
AI-audit / assurance profession, and design every artifact so it can graduate into
paid pilots and regulator partnerships once there is traction.**

Why: open-source is the only zero-cost motion and the category-seeding move (own the
reference implementation / de-facto method); the independent-auditor ecosystem is
the structural growth vector as the EU AI Act (2025–2027) forces third-party
conformity assessment and a financial-audit-style assurance profession forms around it.

## Positioning

- **Category:** verifiable AI-governance auditing (not "another compliance checklist").
- **Wedge:** *every finding is signed, reproducible, and independently verifiable* —
  the thing incumbents (checklist SaaS, slideware audits) can't produce.
- **One-liner:** "Turn AI-governance compliance from PDFs and promises into signed,
  reproducible, court-verifiable evidence."
- Proof it's real: 7-evaluator harness, point-in-time law, warrant-gated chain of
  custody, anti-cherry-pick proofs, real attested probes of frontier models.

## Ideal customer profile (in priority order)

1. **Independent AI auditors / assurance firms & boutiques** (primary). Adopt fastest;
   the tool is their instrument; they *are* the future profession. Pain: they need
   defensible, repeatable methodology and evidence that survives challenge.
2. **Enterprise compliance teams deploying high-risk AI** (the paying demand behind 1):
   hiring, lending, healthcare, ed-tech. Pain: EU AI Act / LL144 / Colorado exposure,
   need pre-deployment due diligence on record.
3. **Regulators / standards bodies** (credibility & long game): align the method with
   what they'll require; not a near-term buyer.

- Or regulator/standards partnership: longest cycle, highest moat.

## Pricing (for later phases — anchors, not commitments)

- Pilot: free → paid pilot ~$5–15k for a scoped audit + signed report.
- Services/retainer: per-audit $10–40k depending on scope (model + data + report).
- SaaS (if built): per-seat for auditors, or per-attested-audit metered.

## Honest risks

- Crowded "AI governance" noise → differentiate hard on *verifiable evidence*, demo it.
- Methodology scrutiny → lean into it; reproducibility + honest null results are the moat.
- Solo bandwidth → open-source + pilots before any platform build; don't pre-build SaaS.
- The institutional ceiling stays: real statutory authority / data-access is not ours
  to grant — sell the tool as the instrument that's *ready when authority exists* and,
  short of that, the most rigorous pre-deployment/assurance auditor available.
